Must-have — do not enter without this

SPY above 200-day SMA must-have

SPY's closing price is above its 200-day simple moving average, and has held there for at least 2–3 consecutive days — not just a single-day spike.

Supporting signals — the more, the better

50-day SMA flattening or rising

The 50-day moving average has stopped declining and is starting to flatten out or curl upward. Signals short-term trend stabilization.

Breadth confirms rally

More than 50% of S&P 500 stocks are above their own 50-day SMA. Rules out a "hollow" rally driven only by a few large tech stocks. (Check $SPXA50R on StockCharts.)

VIX below 20 and declining

The CBOE Volatility Index (fear gauge) has fallen below 20 and isn't spiking back up. Elevated VIX above 25–30 signals ongoing stress.

Higher volume on up days

Green days are seeing noticeably more trading volume than red days in recent weeks. Suggests institutions are buying, not just retail bouncing.

200-day SMA held on retest

After crossing above the 200-day SMA, SPY pulled back to test it and held. Former resistance confirmed as support — a cleaner, lower-risk entry point.

Credit spreads narrowing

High-yield bond spreads are contracting, not widening. Bond markets tend to lead equities — narrowing spreads signal reduced financial stress. (Check HYG or FRED: "ICE BofA High Yield Spread".)
